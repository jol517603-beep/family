陳 · 葉 · 林 · 李 · 邱 · ROOTS OF THE ROSE
1
Tan Keo San 陳橋山
Fujian 1872 — Penang 1941 · founder
Came from China at nineteen with his father's brass watch. Built See Hai Chan 西海棧 on Weld Quay; married into the Beach Street goldsmith family in 1898. Killed when Japanese bombs hit the docks on 11 Dec 1941.
⌚ the brass pocket watch
2
Yap Eng Siew 葉永秀
Beach Street 1880 — 1956 · matriarch
Eldest of the Yap goldsmith sisters — old nyonyas called them 三朵金蘭, the Three Golden Orchids. Wore the IBU kerongsang from her wedding in 1898 to her grave. Drew the chalk line in the kitchen, 1920.
✦ IBU brooch · the mother piece
3
Auntie Mei 美姨
Balik Pulau 1902 — 1998 · the kitchen
A fisherman's daughter brought into the back of the Armenian Street house at eighteen. Wore the same pale jade kebaya for seventy-eight years. Cooked over two hundred Tok Panjang and was never allowed to sit at one. Her full name was finally written down in 1992.
🥢 the empty chair · the locked recipe tin
4
Yap Eng Neo 葉永娘
Beach Street 1882 — 1961 · middle sister
Middle of the Three Golden Orchids. Married Lee Poh Soon, the Penang Road cloth merchant, in 1901. Wore the ANAK A kerongsang. The sister who did a favour once and never spoke of it twice.
✦ ANAK A brooch
5
Yap Eng Hooi 葉永慧
Beach Street 1900 — 1923 · the orchid sister
Youngest of the Three Golden Orchids — born eighteen years after Eng Neo, late and unexpected. Quiet, beautiful, fond of orchids. Never married. Died at twenty-three, winter 1923, in the back of the goldsmith house.
🌸 her brooch was put away
6
Lee Poh Soon 李寶順
Penang Road 1878 — 1948 · cloth merchant
A quiet, unflashy Penang Road cloth merchant. Took over his father's small shop in 1905 and turned it into the most reliable batik source on the north coast. Married Eng Neo in 1901. Folded a length of cream silk for his daughter's wedding kebaya the year before he died.
🧵 the cream silk
7
Tan Cheng Hoe 陳清和
Penang 1920 — 1995 · towkay
Only legitimate son of Keo San & Eng Siew. Inherited See Hai Chan at twenty-one in December 1941. Carried his father's brass watch every day. On his elder son's twenty-first birthday in 1969 he placed it in the boy's palm.
⌚ the watch passes on
8
Lee Beng Guat 李明月
Penang Road 1921 — 2005 · ledger keeper
Daughter of Lee Poh Soon & Eng Neo. Has worn an ANAK B brooch since childhood — a gift from her mother. Married Cheng Hoe 1947: that day her mother-in-law Eng Siew gave her the IBU, her mother gave the ANAK A — and her own ANAK B made the kerongsang complete. Ran the See Hai Chan ledgers for forty-four years.
✦✦✦ the three brooches united
9
Tan Ah Cai 陳亞財
Penang 1925 — ? · the empty plate
Born in the back kitchen, Auntie Mei's only son. The kulis called him Seoy Yee Kia 細姨仔 — "the mistress's son." Walked into the Kulim hills in 1942, came down once in September 1945, walked back up with the men who would not lay down their arms. Never seen again.
🍽 his mother set his place for 53 years
10
Khoo Chin Nam 邱振南
Singapore 1898 — 1949 · shipping
Singapore shipping tycoon, widower at forty-seven. Married young Tan Chen Neo in November 1948 to seal a post-war Penang–Singapore alliance. Died of a heart attack at his own breakfast table eleven months later, August 1949.
⛵ the eleven-month marriage
11
Tan Chen Neo 陳珍娘
Penang 1925 — Singapore 2018 · beadwork
Youngest sister of Cheng Hoe. Crossed the causeway as a Singapore bride at twenty-three, widow eleven months later. Apprenticed at twenty-five to an old Telok Ayer beadwork master and stitched kasut manik for sixty-eight years. Her peony-toe pattern hangs in the Peranakan Museum.
👡 the peony slipper
12
Lee Boon Kuan 李文坤
Penang Road 1918 — 1950 · empty seat
Eldest and only blood son of Lee Poh Soon & Eng Neo. Walked his sister to school every day even when she was old enough to carry her own satchel. Trained as a clerk in his father's shop. Never married. Died of fever, autumn 1950, at thirty-two.
🪑 empty seat at the Lee table
13
Albert Tan 陳順發
Penang 1950 — Singapore 2021 · towkay 2
Younger son of Cheng Hoe & Beng Guat. Sent to Sydney at eighteen, took an English name. Inherited See Hai Chan in 1995, modernised the warehouses, listed on Bursa 2008. Married Alice in 1994 at St Andrew's, Singapore. Died of pancreatic cancer, November 2021.
🏛 the modern boats
14
Alice Wong 王愛麗
KL 1970 — · banker
KL-born, Wharton-educated, Catholic-married. Nyonya by ancestry, English at home, French at school. The first Tan bride in three generations not to wear kebaya at her wedding. Attends every Tok Panjang faithfully — has never entered the kitchen.
📿 a respectful distance
15
Tan Soon Heng 陳順興
Penang 1948 — · architect
Elder of Cheng Hoe & Beng Guat's two sons — the gentler child, always on the kitchen step beside Auntie Mei. Received the brass watch on his 21st birthday in 1969. Restored seventeen Penang shophouses including the family godown. Stroke 2024; watch now on his bedside.
⌚ the watch keeper
16
Lim Soo Hui 林素慧
Ipoh 1966 — · cookbook author
Took the bus from Ipoh at sixteen with one school exercise book and one question for an old kitchen woman: "Auntie, may I write down what your hands do?" Stood beside the chopping block ten years. Married Soon Heng 1990. Resipi Auntie Mei, 1992 — the book that finally named her.
📓 the recipe exercise book
17
Bibik Rose 陳玫瑰
Penang 1974 — · your host
Daughter of Soon Heng & Soo Hui. Trained as a Peranakan textile conservator at the Asian Civilisations Museum, Singapore. The only grandchild in whom the Peranakan culture took root completely — kebaya, kuih, beadwork, prayers. Opened the locked tin in 2024. Tonight is her invitation.
🌹 the one who opened the tin
18
Dr Tan Wei Wen 陳偉文
Penang 1990 — · doctor
Younger child of Soon Heng & Soo Hui — sixteen years younger than his sister. Internal medicine specialist, Penang General Hospital. Met Hooi Wen at a Singapore conference in 2017 and discovered their families had once been arranged into an eleven-month marriage in 1948. Married November 2020.
⚕ closed an old promise
19
Khoo Hooi Wen 邱慧雯
Singapore 1991 — · architect
Great-granddaughter of Khoo Chin Nam through his Singapore first marriage. Architect, NUS. Wore Aunt Chen Neo's never-worn peony-toe kasut manik under her wedding kebaya in November 2020 — seventy-two years after they were stitched.
👡 the slipper completes the conversation
20
Kelvin Tan 陳凱文
Singapore 1995 — · heir
Only son of Albert & Alice. Lives in London, runs the European desk of a logistics fund holding 14% of his own family's company. Twenty-six when his father died in 2021 — signed his first See Hai Chan board paper four days after the wake. Knows the boats. Does not yet know the kitchen.
⚓ takes the chair in 2027
