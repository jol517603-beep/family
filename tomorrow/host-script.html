Tonight · 60 minutes · 5 steps

~ welcome → match → riddle+sew → lock → reveal+toast ~
1
Welcome
~3 min

Stand in front of the whiteboard (title Tan's Family Tree · 陳家根譜 already written across the top). Hold one identity card up. Wait for quiet.

"Selamat datang. Tonight is the Tan family reunion — but not the easy kind. I have twenty faces here, and twenty stories. By the end of one hour, you will know who they are to each other, and who they are to us."
"This board is empty on purpose. You will build the family tree. There is no right shape. The board is yours."
Do: split the room into teams of about 5. Aim for 4 teams. Each team picks a quick name.
2
Round 1 · Match Faces to Stories
~20 min

Hand each team: identity cards #1–#20, story cards A–T, blue tack.

"Round one. Read the stories aloud. Match each story to a face. When you are sure — stick the pair on the whiteboard, in the family tree shape your team thinks is right. Husbands and wives side by side. Children below. You have 20 minutes."
Do: set phone timer for 20 min. Walk between teams. Drop tiny hints only if they're stuck for 5+ min:
"That brooch in story G… have you read story I yet? They are sisters."
"The watch travels through the men of the family — father, son, grandson."
"Auntie Mei never sat at the long table. Where on the tree do you think she belongs?"
At ~10 min, clap once: "Halfway! Ten of twenty done? Keep going."
At buzzer: "Pencils down on matching. Stay where you are — round two is coming."
3
Round 2 · Riddle & Bead Corner
~15 min

Walk to each team. Hand them one riddle card from the deck of 4. Set down a small cloth + needle + thread + beads beside each team.

"Round two. Each team gets one relationship riddle — a long story hidden inside the cards on your board. Crack it together. 15 minutes."
"And the rule — while four of you crack the riddle, one teammate must always be sewing at the bead corner. Pass the needle around. By the end, your team's cloth must have at least 20 beads on it. Riddle + beads, both, or nothing. Hands and head, never separate — that is the Nyonya way."
Do: set timer for 15 min. Walk between teams. Check the bead corner — if a team has nobody sewing, tap one of them on the shoulder and point. Whisper "yes" or "close, try again" when teams whisper their answer. (See answer-key.html, your eyes only.)

The Lock · The Reveal · The Toast

~ the climax, the heart, the close ~
4
Round 3 · Crack the Box
~7 min

When riddle timer ends, clap. Point at the prize box.

"Final round. The box. Three questions, three numbers. First team to whisper the right code, in order, opens it."

Read the 3 questions out loud, one by one. Give 1 minute between each for teams to huddle.

"Question one — how many times did the brass watch change hands?"
"Question two — how many sisters wore the matching brooches?"
"Question three — how many petals on the peony?"
Code = 4 · 3 · 9. First team to whisper "4-3-9" wins. Hand them the box, let them open it dramatically. Inside: red packets / chocolate coins / a family photo. Applause.
If no one gets it in 5 min, give a hint: "Watch crossed four hands. Three sisters. Nine petals." If still no one, just open it yourself: "The code was 4-3-9. The box belongs to all of you."
5
Reveal & Toast
~15 min
total: 3 + 20 + 15 + 7 + 15 = 60 min

Box is open. Everyone is gathered. Turn to the whiteboard.

"Now — let's see what you built. Some of you got it right. Some of you got it beautifully wrong. Both are fine."

Walk through the board. For each person, say two things: their name + one thing that matters about them. Use answer-key.html for letter→number. Move fast — 30 seconds per person.

"#1 Tan Keo San — the reason we are all standing here. Sailed from Fujian at nineteen with a brass watch in his pocket. The watch is still in this family."
"#3 Auntie Mei — cooked for us seventy years. Her name was not in any registry until 1992 when her cookbook was published. Tonight is the first time her face is on the family tree."
"#9 Tan Ah Cai — Auntie Mei's son. He walked into the jungle in 1942 and never came home. The family has waited eighty-four years."

~ keep going for all 20, fast. Don't correct mistakes — just add the truth. ~

Step back. Lower your voice.

"Twenty people. Four generations. Some of them you grew up with. Some you only met tonight, on a piece of paper. All of them are us. We did this not because the names matter — but because the silences matter."
Do: raise a glass. "To the Tans — to the ones we know, and the ones we are still finding." Drink. Done.
