Kerongsang
three-piece nyonya brooch
克朗讓
The item you have is —
Kerongsang · a set of three brooches that pin a nyonya kebaya shut at the throat.

A kerongsang is a traditional, usually three-piece set of decorative brooches used to fasten the front of a Nyonya kebaya or Malay baju kurung. Often made of gold or silver with intricate filigree, it symbolised a family's wealth and heritage.

The Yap sisters' kerongsang — The three Yap sisters of Beach Street goldsmith house were called the Three Golden Orchids 三朵金蘭. Their mother had three gold kerongsang made — the eldest received the Ibu (the big mother-brooch), and the two younger sisters each kept one Anak. A full set is normally two Ibu and three Anak; theirs was the smaller half, deliberately. "No matter how far you walk," she told them, "your sister is your closest backbone."

Jam Saku
gold pocket watch on a chain
袋錶
The item you have is —
Jam Saku · a gold pocket watch on a long chain, the Baba's piece.

Where the nyonya wore her kerongsang at the throat, the Baba wore his jam saku at the waistcoat — chain looped across the front, watch tucked into a small pocket. It told the time of the godown, the wharf and the trading day. A father passed it to his eldest son the night before the wedding — the moment the house was handed over.

Tonight's clue — Tan Towkay's pocket watch still ticks. It has been wound by hand every Sunday morning for one hundred and twenty-eight years.

Cherki
· the only wildcard in the room ·
基里牌
The item you have is —
Cherki · the Peranakan ladies' card game, sixty narrow cards, played for hours after dinner.

Three or four nyonyas, one round table, a small bowl of kuih, a stack of coins, and a deck of cherki cards. The game ran from after dinner until the men came home. The aunties gossiped, scolded, laughed and dealt — and they always pulled in one more chair the moment another cousin walked in.

Tonight's clue — you are the 26th. There are five teams of five — and there is you. Choose any team. Walk over. Sit down. They cannot say no — that is the rule of the cherki table.
Find your team — pick the corner of the long table that needs your laughter most. Sit there.
