Kuih Talam

~ 兩層糕 · the two-layer cake ~
green pandan bottom · white coconut top
From Lim Mei Lan, in her own hand — "Two layers, never one. The green is the earth, the white is the rain. If you rush the bottom, the top will not sit. This is true of cakes, and of people."
Serves: 9 squares Time: 1 hour + cooling Difficulty: easy

Bottom (green pandan)

  • 120 g rice flour
  • 30 g tapioca flour
  • 30 g green pea flour (hoen kwe)
  • 180 g gula melaka, chopped
  • 250 ml fresh pandan juice (blend 8 leaves + 200 ml water, strain)
  • 250 ml water
  • pinch salt

Top (white coconut)

  • 50 g rice flour
  • 20 g tapioca flour
  • 10 g hoen kwe flour
  • 250 ml thick coconut milk (santan)
  • 100 ml water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Method

  1. Bottom: dissolve gula melaka in water on low heat, strain. Mix with pandan juice. Whisk in all bottom flours + salt until smooth. Cook on low, stirring, 5 min until thick like glue. Pour into greased 8" square pan. Steam 20 min on medium.
  2. Top: whisk all top ingredients until smooth. Cook on low, stirring, 3 min until thick. Pour gently over the steamed green layer. Steam another 15 min.
  3. Cool completely (2 hours). Cut with a wet knife into 9 squares.
Mei's note: "Cut clean, or don't cut at all. A messy talam tells the cook was angry."

Kuih Kaya

~ 椰漿蛋糕 · coconut egg jam cake ~
pandan custard layer · sticky glutinous base
From Lim Mei Lan — "Towkay's first wife asked me for this every Sunday for thirty-one years. She never thanked me. But she never asked anyone else either. That is also love."
Serves: 12 squares Time: 1.5 hours Difficulty: easy

Glutinous rice base

  • 300 g glutinous rice, soaked 4 hours
  • 200 ml thin coconut milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted
  • 1 tbsp blue pea flower water (optional, for the marbled look)

Kaya (pandan custard)

  • 5 large eggs
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 250 ml thick coconut milk
  • 120 ml fresh pandan juice (blend 6 leaves + 100 ml water, strain)
  • 40 g rice flour
  • 20 g plain flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Method

  1. Base: drain rice. Mix with coconut milk + salt + pandan leaves. (Drizzle blue pea water in random spots for marbling.) Steam in 8" pan for 25 min until tender. Press flat with wet spoon.
  2. Kaya: whisk eggs + sugar until pale. Add coconut milk + pandan juice. Sift in flours. Strain twice — no lumps allowed.
  3. Pour kaya gently over the rice base. Steam on lowest heat for 40 min, lid wrapped in cloth so no water drips. Toothpick should come out clean.
  4. Cool 2 hours. Cut into squares.
Mei's note: "Strain the kaya twice or your guests will know. They will not say it. But they will know."

Kuih Lapis

~ 九層糕 · the nine-layer cake ~
pink · white · pink · white · nine times
From Lim Mei Lan — "Nine layers, like the peony on my apron. Nine layers, like the petals of patience. The trick is not the colour — it is the waiting between each pour. Don't rush. The cake remembers."
Serves: 12 strips Time: 1 hour 45 min Difficulty: easy (but slow)

Single batter

  • 250 g rice flour
  • 100 g tapioca flour
  • 50 g hoen kwe flour
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 500 ml thick coconut milk
  • 500 ml water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted

To divide

  • Split batter in half:
  • Pink half — add 4-5 drops red food colour (or beetroot juice)
  • White half — leave plain
  • You will need 9 layers total: pink-white-pink-white-pink-white-pink-white-pink

Method

  1. Heat coconut milk + water + sugar + salt + pandan leaves until sugar dissolves. Cool fully. Remove leaves.
  2. Whisk all flours into the cooled liquid. Strain. Divide in half. Colour one half pink.
  3. Grease 8" square pan. Steam empty for 5 min on high.
  4. Pour 1 ladle of pink into hot pan. Steam 5 min on high. Pour 1 ladle of white. Steam 5 min. Repeat — alternate pink, white — until you have 9 layers. (Total ~45 min steaming.)
  5. Final layer steam 10 min. Cool 2 hours. Cut into long strips. Eat with fingers. Peel one layer at a time.
Mei's note: "If the layers blur, your steam was too low. If they crack, too high. Find the middle. Like the middle sister."

Kuih Dadar

~ 椰絲卷餅 · ketayap · pandan rolls with coconut ~
green crepe wrapping warm gula melaka coconut
From Lim Mei Lan — "Ah Cai loved this one. He would eat six in a row, standing by the back door. I made it the morning he left for the jungle in 1942. He took two with him in a banana leaf. He never came back. I still make six every Sunday."
Serves: 10 rolls Time: 45 min Difficulty: easy

Pandan crepe

  • 120 g plain flour
  • 1 tbsp tapioca flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 180 ml fresh pandan juice (6 leaves + 150 ml water, strained)
  • 120 ml thin coconut milk
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil + extra for pan

Inti (coconut filling)

  • 200 g freshly grated coconut (white part only)
  • 120 g gula melaka, chopped fine
  • 50 ml water
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted
  • pinch salt

Method

  1. Inti: melt gula melaka + water + pandan leaves on low heat until syrupy. Strain. Toss with coconut + salt in a wok over very low heat 5 min until just sticky, not dry. Cool.
  2. Crepe: whisk egg + pandan juice + coconut milk. Add salt and oil. Sift in flours. Whisk smooth. Strain. Rest 15 min.
  3. Heat a small non-stick pan, lightly oiled. Pour ~2 tbsp batter, swirl thin. Cook 30 sec — top should look matte. Slide off onto plate. Repeat.
  4. Place 1 tbsp inti at one edge of each crepe. Fold sides in, roll like a spring roll. Serve warm or room temp.
Mei's note: "Don't flip the crepe. One side cooked is enough. The other side should remember it was once batter."

Kuih Bingka Ubi

~ 木薯糕 · baked tapioca cake ~
golden caramelised top · soft chewy middle
From Lim Mei Lan — "The only kuih in this house I baked, not steamed. The towkay's second daughter said it tasted like the sun. I told her the sun was free, and so was this. I lied. The eggs were not free. But the recipe is now."
Serves: 12 squares Time: 1 hour 15 min Difficulty: easy

Ingredients

  • 500 g grated tapioca (fresh or thawed frozen, well-drained)
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 250 ml thick coconut milk
  • 50 g melted butter
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla (or 2 pandan leaves blended in)
  • 1 tbsp tapioca flour (if mixture too wet)

For the golden top

  • 2 tbsp coconut milk
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • pinch salt

Brushed on at 30-min mark to caramelise.

Method

  1. Pre-heat oven to 175°C. Grease 8" square pan.
  2. Squeeze grated tapioca well — get rid of all excess water. (Watery tapioca = sad bingka.)
  3. Whisk eggs + sugar + salt until pale. Add coconut milk, melted butter, vanilla. Fold in tapioca and tapioca flour.
  4. Pour into pan. Bake 30 min — top should be just setting.
  5. Brush top with glaze (coconut milk + sugar + salt). Bake another 20-25 min until top is deep golden brown and glossy.
  6. Cool fully (1 hour minimum) before cutting. The middle firms up as it cools.
Mei's note: "If the top is pale, you took it out too early. A bingka with no colour is a bingka with no story."
