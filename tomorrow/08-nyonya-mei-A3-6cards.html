Nyonya Mei

林梅蘭
LIM MEI LAN · 1908 – 1996
She was not born a Tan. She married no Tan. No grandchild carried her name. But for fifty-one years she stood at the back kitchen of the house on Lebuh Aceh, stirring santan, folding pandan, layering kuih nine times over — while the family upstairs ate, and laughed, and forgot her name.
Today we remember.
These are her five recipes.

Kuih Talam

兩層糕 · the two-layer cake
green pandan below · white coconut above
Two layers, never one. The green is the earth, the white is the rain.
serves9time1h+leveleasy

Ingredients

Bottom · pandan
  • 120 g rice flour
  • 30 g tapioca flour
  • 30 g hoen kwe
  • 180 g gula melaka
  • 250 ml pandan juice
  • 250 ml water · pinch salt
Top · coconut
  • 50 g rice flour · 20 g tapioca
  • 10 g hoen kwe
  • 250 ml thick santan
  • 100 ml water · ½ tsp salt

Method

  1. Bottom: melt gula melaka in water, strain. Mix with pandan juice. Whisk in flours + salt. Cook on low ~5 min until thick.
  2. Steam: pour into greased 8" pan. Steam 20 min on medium.
  3. Top: whisk all top ingredients. Cook on low ~3 min until thick.
  4. Layer & steam: pour gently over green. Steam 15 min more.
  5. Cool 2 h. Cut into 9 squares with a wet knife.
Mei's noteCut clean, or don't cut at all. A messy talam tells the cook was angry.

Kuih Kaya

椰漿蛋糕 · coconut egg jam cake
pandan custard above · glutinous rice below
Towkay's first wife asked me for this every Sunday for thirty-one years. That is also love.
serves12time1½hleveleasy

Ingredients

Glutinous base
  • 300 g glutinous rice, soaked 4 h
  • 200 ml thin santan
  • ½ tsp salt · 2 pandan leaves
  • 1 tbsp blue pea water (optional)
Pandan custard
  • 5 large eggs
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 250 ml thick santan
  • 120 ml pandan juice
  • 40 g rice flour · 20 g plain
  • ¼ tsp salt

Method

  1. Base: drain rice. Mix with santan, salt, pandan. Steam in 8" pan 25 min. Press flat.
  2. Custard: whisk eggs + sugar pale. Add santan + pandan juice. Sift flours. Strain twice.
  3. Layer: pour gently over rice base.
  4. Slow steam on lowest heat 40 min, lid wrapped in cloth (no drips).
  5. Cool 2 h. Slice with wet knife.
Mei's noteStrain the kaya twice or your guests will know. They will not say it. But they will know.

Kuih Lapis

九層糕 · the nine-layer cake
pink · white · nine times over
Nine layers, like the peony on my apron. The trick is not the colour — it is the waiting.
serves12time1¾hlevelslow

Ingredients

Single batter
  • 250 g rice flour
  • 100 g tapioca · 50 g hoen kwe
  • 250 g caster sugar
  • 500 ml thick santan
  • 500 ml water · 1 tsp salt
  • 2 pandan leaves, knotted
To divide
  • Half: 4–5 drops red colour
  • Half: leave plain (white)
  • Pour 9 layers total: P-W-P-W-P-W-P-W-P

Method

  1. Syrup: heat santan + water + sugar + salt + pandan until dissolved. Cool fully.
  2. Batter: whisk in all flours. Strain. Divide. Colour one half pink.
  3. Heat pan: grease 8" pan, steam empty 5 min on high.
  4. Layer by layer: 1 ladle pink, steam 5 min. 1 ladle white, steam 5 min. Repeat — 9 layers, ~45 min total.
  5. Final 10 min steam. Cool 2 h. Cut strips. Eat with fingers.
Mei's noteIf layers blur, steam too low. If they crack, too high. Find the middle. Like the middle sister.

Kuih Dadar

椰絲卷餅 · ketayap
pandan crepe rolled around gula melaka coconut
Ah Cai took two with him in a banana leaf in 1942. He never came back. I still make six every Sunday.
serves10time45mleveleasy

Ingredients

Pandan crepe
  • 120 g plain flour · 1 tbsp tapioca
  • 1 large egg
  • 180 ml pandan juice
  • 120 ml thin santan
  • ¼ tsp salt · 1 tsp oil
Inti · filling
  • 200 g grated coconut
  • 120 g gula melaka, fine
  • 50 ml water
  • 2 pandan leaves · pinch salt

Method

  1. Inti first: melt gula melaka with water + pandan, strain. Toss with coconut + salt on low heat 5 min — sticky, not dry. Cool.
  2. Batter: whisk egg + pandan + santan. Add salt + oil. Sift flours. Strain. Rest 15 min.
  3. Crepe: small oiled non-stick pan. 2 tbsp batter, swirl thin. Cook 30 sec — matte top. Slide off. Don't flip.
  4. Roll: 1 tbsp inti at one edge. Fold sides, roll like spring roll.
  5. Serve warm or room temp.
Mei's noteDon't flip. One side cooked is enough. The other should remember it was once batter.

Kuih Bingka Ubi

木薯糕 · baked tapioca cake
deep golden top · soft chewy middle
Towkay's daughter said it tasted like the sun. I told her the sun was free, and so was this. I lied.
serves12time1¼hleveleasy

Ingredients

Cake
  • 500 g grated tapioca, drained
  • 200 g caster sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 250 ml thick santan
  • 50 g melted butter
  • ½ tsp salt · 1 tsp vanilla
  • 1 tbsp tapioca flour (if wet)
Glaze
  • 2 tbsp santan
  • 1 tsp sugar · pinch salt

Method

  1. Heat oven 175 °C. Grease 8" pan.
  2. Drain well: squeeze tapioca thoroughly. Watery tapioca = sad bingka.
  3. Mix: whisk eggs + sugar + salt pale. Add santan, butter, vanilla. Fold in tapioca + flour.
  4. Bake 30 min — top should just be setting.
  5. Glaze & finish: brush with glaze. Bake 20–25 min more until deep golden + glossy.
  6. Cool 1 h before cutting.
Mei's noteIf the top is pale, you took it out too early. A bingka with no colour is a bingka with no story.
