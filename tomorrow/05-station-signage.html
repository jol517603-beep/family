Batik

~ paint your square · take it home ~
★ 60 MIN · joget begins at 4:10 sharp ★

What you'll get

One small white cotton square (about 25 × 25 cm), already stretched on a wooden frame, with the wax outline of a Peranakan motif drawn on it: peony, hibiscus, butterfly, phoenix, or geometric tile. Pick one. The wax keeps your colours from running.

How to paint

1
Pick your colours
3-4 max · less is more
2
Dip brush, dab once on cloth
remove excess so it doesn't bleed
3
Paint inside the wax lines
the wax stops the colour
4
Let dry on the rack
~15 min · take home in tube

Tips from the helper

Start light, build dark. You can always add more colour. You can never take it out.
Don't paint over wet paint — it muddies. Wait or move to another area of the cloth.
Mistake? Make it part of the design. Peranakans don't waste cloth.
Sign your square in the bottom corner — small, in pencil. We'll add the date.

Joget

~ everyone dances · no exceptions ~
★ 20 MIN · the closing ★

The dance, simply

Joget is a fast, flirtatious Malay social dance — quick footwork, gentle swaying hands, eye contact, smiles. You do not need to know the steps. The point is movement together, not technique.

Bibi will lead. Form a loose circle. Pair up if you want, or dance solo in the circle — both are joget. Music will be three songs: a slow one to warm up, a fast one to commit, a slow one to close.

The 4 simple steps

1
Step right · tap left
on the beat
2
Step left · tap right
mirror
3
Hands at waist height
soft wrists, palms turning out and in
4
Smile · make eye contact
that is the whole dance

The closing

When the third song ends, Bibi calls everyone to the centre. Big group photo with all 5 AI portraits held up. Then guests leave with three things:

1. Their team's recipe card (cook it, teach it, spread it).
2. Their batik square (signed and dated, in a tube to take home).
3. Their Selphy AI portrait (handed out at the door on the way out).

Selamat tinggal, sampai jumpa lagi.
