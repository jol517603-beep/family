📍 OUTSIDE-TREE · SLOT 1
1
Kamcheng Mas Kahwin
金水饺甘望
🥟 The bride's first-day jar · gold-dumpling kamcheng
On her wedding morning, the new Nyonya carries this kamcheng across the threshold of her husband's house. Inside: nine gold-glazed sweet dumplings — one for each generation she promises to feed. From the moment her foot crosses the door, no in-law sits above her in the kitchen. The house, from this morning, is hers.
🔗 mentioned only as a passing whisper in Card #2 Yap Eng Siew — “the eldest carried the gold dumplings in.”
📍 OUTSIDE-TREE · SLOT 2
2
Kamcheng Tok Panjang
长桌甘望
🌶️ The centre-of-the-feast jar · sambal & achar
For twelve days of Tok Panjang — the long-table feast — this kamcheng holds the sambal belacan and achar. Whoever lifts its lid is the host. Whoever refills it is the cook. The two are almost never the same person… and that, in our family, is its own quiet story.
🔗 mentioned in Card #3 Lim Mei Lan (“cooked every tok panjang”) and Card #7 Tan Cheng Hoe (head of house, lifted the lid).
📍 OUTSIDE-TREE · SLOT 3
3
Kamcheng Cuci Mulut
甜尾甘望
🍯 The vow-keeper jar · sealed at engagement, opened at the wedding
On the day of betrothal, the matriarch fills it with gula melaka kueh and seals the lid with red wax. It sits on the family altar, untouched, until the wedding banquet — sometimes for a year, sometimes for ten. To open it early is to break the vow. Every Tan-Lim wedding ends with a slice from this jar.
🔗 mentioned in Card #18 Wei Wen + Card #19 Hooi Wen (the 2020 wedding) — “the wax was finally cracked after seventy-two years.”

🍵 The 3 Kamcheng · BACK side · the function in a Peranakan house
🥟 KAMCHENG MAS KAHWIN
1
🥟

Why nine gold dumplings?

In a Peranakan household, the kitchen ranks higher than the parlour. Whoever owns the kitchen owns the house. The bride's first-day kamcheng is her declaration of kitchen authority. Nine dumplings = nine generations she is now responsible for feeding (3 above her, 1 alongside, 5 to come).

Function

Carried only once in a lifetime. Then washed, sealed, and kept on the highest kitchen shelf for the next bride.

Today

Most modern Nyonya brides skip it. The Tan house never did — until now. Bibi has not yet decided who carries the next one. 🌺

🌶️ KAMCHENG TOK PANJANG
2
🌶️

The lid and the ladle.

Tok Panjang means “long table.” On Cap Goh Mei, weddings, and full-month celebrations, the kamcheng of sambal & achar sits at the centre. The lid is lifted by the host; the ladle is held by the cook. Two roles, two people, never confused.

Function

It marks who serves and who is served. In our story, one woman cooked and never sat. One man sat and never cooked. The kamcheng remembers both.

Today

The Tan tok panjang kamcheng has a small chip on the lid — from 1968, the year Mei Lan dropped it crying. Bibi never had it repaired. 🌺

🍯 KAMCHENG CUCI MULUT
3
🍯

A sealed promise.

Cuci mulut means “rinse the mouth” — the sweet course that closes a Peranakan banquet. This kamcheng is sealed with red wax at engagement and may not be opened until the wedding day. To break the wax early is to break the marriage promise.

Function

It is the family's contract in porcelain. The longer it sits sealed, the deeper the vow. Some Tan kamcheng sit twelve months. Aunt Chen Neo's sat seventy-two years — sealed at her engagement to the Singapore tycoon in 1948, never opened in her short marriage. Her step-great-granddaughter Hooi Wen broke its wax at the 2020 wedding.

Today

Empty now. Bibi keeps it on the altar with a fresh stick of incense every Sunday. 🌺

📜 Each Kamcheng is a quiet rule of the house — not a person, but a function the family revolves around. 🌺
