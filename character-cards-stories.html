🌺 Roots of the Rose · The Story Deck 🌺

20 character cards · 4 rounds · the family tree, the heirlooms, and the Yap secret

🎯 ROUND 1 — Match the Character · read the story 💬 → find the silhouette → place the card.
📝 ROUND 1.5 — Bibi's Note · one folded note from Bibi, full of riddles. She never names a relationship. Use her clues to fill the 5 Special Relationships column on your worksheet.
🟡 ROUND 2 — Place the 10 Heirlooms · the envelope holds 10 items. Decide which go in the 3 tree circles, the 3 outside-tree column, and which 2 are tucked inside character cards.
🤫 ROUND 3 — Crack the Yap Secret · two folded secret notes hide inside two of the three Yap sisters' cards (#2 Eng Siew & #4 Eng Neo). Find them. Read them. Write what they reveal.
👠 ROUND 4 — Sew the Kasut Manik · in tribute to Aunt Chen Neo. Team of 5 takes the bead kit, follows the pattern, finishes the slipper toe. A hidden number appears in the finished beadwork — write it on the worksheet.
Watch for the 🤫 stamp — it whispers where to look. Watch for the 👠 stamp — that's the bead-sister.
Gen 1 · The Founders (1872 – 1948)
1
Tan Keo San
陳橋山
1872 – 1941 · sinkeh from Fujian
S1·⌚Stepped off a Fujian junk in 1891 with a brass pocket watch his father pressed into his palm: «time is the only thing nobody can steal back from you.» Built See Hai Chan 西海棧 from porter to towkay in 30 years. On 11 Dec 1941 he walked to Weld Quay to meet a delayed lighter — the first Japanese bomb fell at 11:14. The watch came home in his coat pocket, still ticking. ⌚

🤫 sister
Yap Eng Siew
葉永秀
1880 – 1956 · eldest Yap sister
S2·📌Eldest of three Yap sisters. Married Keo San in 1898 wearing the Ibu (母) kerongsang. Drew a chalk line on the kitchen floor when her husband brought a second woman home — and kept her sister's secret of 1923 until the day she died. Note ① is folded inside this card. 🤫
3
🤫 named
Lim Mei Lan
林美蘭
1902 – 1998 · "Auntie Mei" 美姑
S3·📜Brought into the back of the Tan house at 18. Cooked over 200 wedding feasts — never sat at one. Wore one pale-jade kebaya for 78 years with a permanent sambal stain on the hem. Bore one son. Her own brother's daughter walked into her kitchen at sixteen and finally wrote her name. Died at 96. 🌿
4
🤫 sister
Yap Eng Neo
葉永娘
1882 – 1961 · middle Yap sister
S2·📌Middle of three Yap sisters. Married into the Lee house wearing the Anak A (子) kerongsang. One winter dawn in 1923 a midwife knocked at her door with a baby wrapped in temple cloth — and she took the child in without telling her own husband what she had done. Note ② is folded inside this card. 🤫

died young ✝
Yap Eng Hooi
葉永慧
1900 – 1923 · youngest Yap sister
S2·✝The orchid sister — youngest, beautiful, never married. Pregnant by a Bishop Street tea-clerk in 1922, hidden behind a screen in the goldsmith back room for seven months, gone before sunrise on a December night in 1923. The baby left in a basket of temple cloth. The Anak B brooch — engraved 1900 — was polished but never worn again. ✝
6
Lee Poh Soon
李寶順
1878 – 1948 · Lee patriarch
S2Quiet cloth merchant on Penang Road. Married Eng Neo in 1901. One winter morning in 1923 his wife came home before sunrise carrying an infant girl. He asked one question — «whose child?» — accepted the answer, and never asked again. Folded a length of cream silk for her bridal kebaya in 1948 and wrote on the receipt: «for the second daughter — paid in full.»
Gen 2 · The Inheritors (1918 – 2005)
7
Tan Cheng Hoe
陳清和
1920 – 1995 · only legitimate son
S5·⌚The watch came home in his father's coat in Dec 1941. He inherited See Hai Chan at 21 and ran it for 47 years. Married a Lee girl in 1947 — never knew his mother had quietly arranged him a marriage with his own first cousin. On his youngest son's 21st birthday in 1969 he placed the brass watch in the boy's palm: «wind it in the morning. don't forget.» ⌚→
8
🤫 cousin
Lee Beng Guat
李明月
1921 – 2005 · daughter (in name) of the Lees
S5·📌Born in the back room of the goldsmith house, carried before dawn to the door of her aunt and uncle Lee. Grew up Lee, married Tan in 1947 — never told her wedding was, by blood, between cousins. Ran the See Hai Chan ledgers for 44 years. The night before she died she handed her granddaughter Rose a brass key and said: «open the tin when you are ready.» 🤫
9
🤫 jungle
Tan Ah Cai
陳亞財
1925 – ? · the kitchen son
S3·🌿Born in the Tan kitchen to Auntie Mei. Never sent to school. Mingled with the kulis at Weld Quay — they called him Seoy Yee Kia 細姨仔, "the mistress's son." Joined the MPAJA when the Japanese came. Walked back into the jungle with the men who refused the British surrender terms in Sept 1945. The family has waited 81 years. His mother set a place at every Tok Panjang. 🌿
10
Khoo Chin Nam
邱振南
1898 – 1949 · Singapore tycoon
S4The Singapore shipping tycoon — already a widower with a grown son when, in November 1948, the families arranged a Penang–Singapore alliance. He married Tan Chen Neo, then 23. Eleven months later his heart stopped at his own breakfast table. The Singapore branch continued through his first-marriage son — and one great-granddaughter from that line would walk back into the story 72 years later. ⛵

👠 manik
Tan Chen Neo
陳珍娘
1925 – 2018 · the bead-sister
S4·👠Cheng Hoe's youngest sister. Married off to the Singapore tycoon at 23 — «a Nyonya daughter does not say no.» Widowed eleven months later. Refused to come home. Apprenticed herself to her stepson's mother-in-law and learned kasut manik. Became, by 60, the named master of three Singapore showrooms. Returned the 1948 wedding slippers to her step-great-granddaughter Hooi Wen in 2018. 🌸👠

李文坤
1918 – 1950 · Lee firstborn
✝Eldest blood son of the Lees. Five years older than the small sister who arrived one morning wrapped in temple cloth — and the only person in the household who never wondered where she had come from. He simply called her mei mei and walked her to school. Took ill in 1950, three years after walking her down the aisle. Died at 32 of a fever that took three days. ✝
Gen 3 · The Two Branches (1948 – today)
13
d. 2021
Albert Tan
陳順發
1950 – 2021 · towkay of See Hai Chan
S6Younger Tan son. Took the English name Albert at university in Sydney. Inherited See Hai Chan at 45 when his father died, listed it on Bursa in 2008. Wore Italian shoes, spoke Hokkien only to suppliers. The Peranakan things — the kebaya, the kuih, the prayers — passed cleanly past him. Died of pancreatic cancer in 2021 at 71. Left the boats to his son Kelvin and the brooches to his brother. 🚢

Throws Christmas dinner at the KL house every year. Attends Bibi's Tok Panjang faithfully — drinks tea in the parlour, never enters the kitchen. Widowed since 2021. ✚

⌚ watch
Tan Soon Heng
陳順興
1948 – today · heritage architect
S7·⌚Elder, gentler Tan son. The family rule said the watch goes to the youngest — but his father had quietly amended the rule before either boy was old enough to ask: the watch goes to the gentler one. Took the brass jam saku on his 21st birthday in 1969 and wound it every morning at six for 55 years. Restored 17 Penang shophouses. Stroke in 2024 — the watch now sits on a porcelain dish on his bedside table. Rose's father. ⌚🌺

林素慧

S7·📜Daughter of Auntie Mei's only brother. Walked into the Tan kitchen from Ipoh at 16 with a school exercise book and one question: «Auntie, may I write down what your hands do?» Stood beside the chopping block for 10 years. In 1992 published «Resipi Auntie Mei» — dedication: «To Auntie Mei (Lim Mei Lan 林美蘭) — the woman the family forgot to name.» Married Soon Heng in 1990. 📜🌿

📋 Designer notes — the 4 rounds + the heirloom story arcs:

🎯 ROUND 1 — Match. 20 cards, each with a story 💬. Match → silhouette → place. Story tags S1–S8 mirror the bubbles on the family-tree board.

📝 ROUND 1.5 — Bibi's Note (1 card). A single folded note packed with riddles. Bibi never names anyone. Players use her hints to fill the 5 Special Relationships column on the team worksheet:
  ① step-mother (Chen Neo → Khoo Chin Nam's grown son) · ② hidden son (Mei Lan → Ah Cai) · ③ raised-by aunt (Eng Hooi's baby → Eng Neo) · ④ watch-keeper by gentleness (Soon Heng holds it, not his younger brother Albert) · ⑤ kitchen mentor & blood (Mei Lan → Soo Hui, who turned out to be her own niece).

🟡 ROUND 2 — The 10 envelope items.
3 go in the tree circles: 📌 Kerongsang stack (3 brooches → ONE circle bridging cards #2 · #4 · #5) · ⌚ Jam Saku (Keo San → Cheng Hoe → Soon Heng) · 📜 Resipi Auntie Mei (1992 cookbook).
3 go in the outside-tree column (heritage enhancers): a Fujian dock photo, the See Hai Chan godown brass plate, the kamcheng of nine gold dumplings.
2 are tucked inside character cards as folded inserts (the 2 Yap secret notes — Round 3, inside #2 Eng Siew & #4 Eng Neo).
2 are red herrings — a beautiful bunga rampai sachet and a chipped sambal jar lid that look heirloom-y but belong nowhere. (Discard pile.)

🤫 ROUND 3 — The Yap Secret. Two folded notes inside two of the three Yap-sister cards: ① in #2 Eng Siew (the eldest, who arranged the cover-up), ② in #4 Eng Neo (the middle, who took the baby in). Card #5 Eng Hooi has nothing tucked in her — she is the secret.

👠 ROUND 4 — Kasut Manik. Team of 5 receives a small bead kit and a pattern grid based on Aunt Chen Neo's signature peony toe. Each member sews a section. When complete, the finished pattern reveals a hidden number woven in a contrasting bead colour. Write the number on the worksheet — it's the lock code for Bibi's final envelope.

📌 KERONGSANG arc (#2 · #4 · #5): Ibu (母) with eldest Eng Siew → Anak A (子) with middle Eng Neo → Anak B (engraved 1900) belonged to youngest Eng Hooi, polished but never worn. Stack all 3 into ONE tree circle.
⌚ JAM SAKU arc (#1 · #7 · #15): Fujian father → Keo San (1872, sailed 1891) → Cheng Hoe (1941, jetty bomb) → Soon Heng on his 21st (1969) → bedside dish (2024).
📜 RESIPI arc (#3 · #16): Mei Lan cooked everything, sat at nothing. Her own niece Soo Hui spent 10 years in her kitchen, wrote 240 dishes, dedicated the cookbook in her name. Mei Lan saw it published and lived 6 more good years.

🌿 SILENCE arc (#3 · #9): The fourth thread no one has pulled — Mei Lan and her son Ah Cai, the kitchen line that vanished into the jungle in Sept 1945. The empty plate is still on the kitchen shelf. Bibi sets it out at every Cap Goh Mei.

🎨 Print: A4 portrait, 4 cards × 5 rows. Cut along card borders. Cards #2 and #4 need a fold-line down the middle so a small insert tucks inside without falling out. Card #11 Chen Neo carries the 👠 stamp — she's your bead mistress.
