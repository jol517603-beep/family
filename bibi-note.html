Hints · not Answers

I will not name a single person. I am not allowed to.

But every line I whisper here fits next to a line on a card. When the card and my whisper meet, an answer falls out. That is how secrets travel in a Nyonya house — never alone, never direct.

How to play: on the worksheet there is a column called “5 Special Relationships”. Each of my five riddles points to one. Look at the cards you placed. Find the line that completes mine. Then write the names in.

Five Whispers from Bibi 🌺

Each whisper meets one card · together they name a relationship
①
A girl of twenty-three was sent south in a wedding palanquin in November 1948. The man waiting for her at the Singapore pier had grown sons of his own. Eleven months later she sat in a white kebaya at his table — and on the other side sat a man older than herself who, by paper, was now her child. She never corrected him when he called her by her given name.
Pair me with: the bead-sister card and the Singapore tycoon card. The relationship column = ?
②
She cooked for two hundred weddings and was never seated at one. There was a boy who slept on a mat by the back stove and never went to school. The dock kulis called him «the small auntie's boy». He walked into the jungle with the resistance in September of forty-five and the empty plate on the kitchen shelf has been hers ever since.
Pair me with: the kitchen woman in the pale-jade kebaya and the boy whose dates end with a question mark. The relationship column = ?
③
Mother Yap had three daughters. Only two ever stood at an altar. The third left a cradle behind on a December dawn in 1923. The cradle was carried into the Lee house and rocked by hands that called the baby «ours». The brooch under her wedding kebaya was small and gold-edged. The auntie who fastened it said only: «this was your mother's.»
Pair me with: the middle Yap sister card and the daughter who married into the Tans in 1947. The relationship column = ?
④
Two brothers stood in their father's study on a 21st birthday. By the old rule the brass thing should have gone to the youngest. But the father had quietly amended the rule the year both boys were small: «it goes to the gentler one.» The elder wound it every morning at six for fifty-five years. The younger built a shipping empire and never once asked for it back.
Pair me with: the heritage-architect father and the late towkay brother. The relationship column = ?
⑤
A sixteen-year-old girl arrived from Ipoh in 1982 with a school exercise book and one question: «Auntie, may I write down what your hands do?» She sat on the low stool for ten years. In 1992 she published a cookbook and dedicated it to «the woman the family forgot to name.» What the family did not yet know — what only the auntie knew — was that the girl was her own brother's daughter.
Pair me with: the kitchen woman in the pale-jade kebaya and the wife of the heritage architect. The relationship column = ?
