Aunt Chen Neo's Peony

~ one bead each · one petal at a time · no skipping ~

How to sew

  1. Sit at the bead corner in turn order (clockwise).
  2. Each player sews one row of beads for one petal.
  3. Pass the cloth to the next player. No skipping turns.
  4. The team finishes when the peony has 9 petals.

What to count

  • The petals are the orchid sisters and their lines.
  • The centre is the founder.
  • When the flower is whole, count the petals — that number is № 3 of the lock.
"Every bead is a stitch in time.
If you cut the thread, the flower forgets." — Tan Chen Neo, 1972
