Tonight, in 7 Easy Steps

~ ~60 minutes · banner board · cards · riddles · bead corner · lockbox ~
1
Welcome & Set the Scene
~3 min

Stand in front of the foam board. The banner (“Tan's Family Tree · 陳家根譜”) is already taped across the top — the rest of the board is empty. Hold one identity card in your hand. Wait for the room to quiet.

"Selamat datang. Tonight is the Tan family reunion — but not the easy kind. I have twenty faces here, and twenty stories. Some of these people you know. Some you will meet for the first time. By the end of tonight, you will know who they are to each other — and who they are to us."
"This board is empty on purpose. Only the name is up there. You will build the family tree underneath it. There is no right shape. Husbands and wives side by side. Children below. Brothers and sisters in a row. The board is yours."
Do: tap the banner with your hand. Smile. Hold up one identity card so they see what it looks like.
2
Hand Out the Cards
~4 min

Split the room into teams of 5. Aim for 4 teams. Each team picks a name on the spot (anything — "Team Brooch", "Team Watch", whatever).

Give each team:

  • 1 set of identity cards #1–#20 (faces + names)
  • 1 set of story cards A–T (the long stories)
  • Push pins or tape
"Round one is simple: match faces to stories. Read the stories aloud. Look at the faces. When you are sure who is who — pin the pair on the board, in the family tree shape your team thinks is right. You have 20 minutes for this round."
"After matching, I will come around with a relationship riddle card for each team — and the lockbox is on my table. We'll get there. First: match."
Do: set a phone timer for 20 min. Walk away. Let them work. Refill drinks.
3
Round 1 · Match the Cards
~20 min

Walk between teams. Don't give answers. Drop tiny hints only when a team is stuck for more than 5 minutes.

"That brooch in story G… have you read story I yet? They are sisters."
"The watch travels through the men of the family — father, son, grandson. Three names."
"Auntie Mei never sat at the long table. Where on the tree do you think she belongs?"
Do: if a team gets a match clearly right, give them one gold coin (chocolate or otherwise). Keep score loose. At ~10 min, clap once: "Halfway! Ten of twenty done? Keep going." When the timer ends, clap and say: "Pencils down on matching. Time for round two."

Round 2 & The Lock

~ this is where the game gets interesting ~
4
Round 2 · The Relationship Riddle (and the Bead Corner)
~15 min

Walk to each team. Hand them one relationship clue card from the printed deck (Card 1 to Team A, Card 2 to Team B, etc.). Set down a small 串珠 panel + needle + thread + beads at the bead corner for each team.

"Round two. Each team gets one relationship riddle — a long story hidden inside the cards on your board. Read it together. Talk through it. Find the people on the board it is talking about, and pin them with a string. You have 15 minutes."
"And — here is the rule. While four of you crack the riddle, one person from your team must go to the bead corner and sew. You can rotate. Pass the needle around. By the time the riddle is solved, your team's 串珠 panel must have at least 20 beads on it. Both, or nothing. That is the Nyonya way — hands and head, never separate."
"When you have an answer, whisper it to me. Don't shout — the other teams might be onto something different."
Do: set a phone timer for 15 min. Walk between teams quietly. Check the bead corner — if a team has nobody sewing, gently tap one of them on the shoulder and point. Riddle answers are on page 3 of this script (your eyes only). Whisper "yes" or "close, try again" when they whisper to you. Each correct riddle = 5 gold coins to the team.
5
Round 3 · Crack the Lock
~5 min

When the 15-min riddle timer ends, clap once. Point at the lockbox.

"Final round. The box. Three missions, three numbers, in order. First team to whisper the right code opens it."
  • Mission 1 · The Watch — how many times the brass watch changed hands  → 4
  • Mission 2 · The Brooches — how many sisters wore them  → 3
  • Mission 3 · The Beads — peony petals in the bead corner  → 9
Lock code = 4 · 3 · 9. When a team gets it, hand them the lock, let them open it dramatically. Inside: red packets / chocolate coins / a printed family photo. Applause. Move straight into the Reveal.

The Reveal, The Close, The Answers

~ the heart of the night, then the toast ~
6
The Reveal
~10 min

With the box open and everyone gathered — turn to the foam board.

"Now let's see what you built. Some of you got it right. Some of you got it beautifully wrong. Both are fine."

Walk through the board character by character. Don't correct mistakes — just add the truth.

"#1 Tan Keo San — the reason we are all standing here. He sailed from Fujian at nineteen with a brass watch in his pocket. The watch is still in this family."
"#3 Auntie Mei — she cooked for us for seventy years. Her name was not in any registry until 1992 when her cookbook was published. Tonight is the first time her face is on the family tree."
"#9 Tan Ah Cai — Auntie Mei's son. He walked into the jungle in 1942 and never came home. The family has waited eighty-four years."

~ keep going for all 20. Use story-cards.html page 5 (letter → number key) as your cheat sheet. ~

7
The Close · Why We Did This
~3 min
total: 3 + 4 + 20 + 15 + 5 + 10 + 3 = 60 min
"Look at this tree. Twenty people. Four generations. All of them are us."
"We did this not because the names matter — but because the silences matter. Auntie Mei. Ah Cai. The Yap sisters. They were here all along. Tonight we say their names out loud."
Do: raise a small glass. "To the Tans — to the ones we know, and the ones we are still finding." Drink. Done.

Riddle Answer Key · Bibi's eyes only
do NOT show teams

Card 1 · The Boy in the Storeroom — The boy is #9 Tan Ah Cai. His real father is #1 Tan Keo San (Auntie Mei was the kept second woman, not just the cook). That makes Ah Cai the half-brother of Cheng Hoe — a Tan by blood, even though the registry says Lim. Acceptable answer: “Keo San is the father. Ah Cai is a Tan, not a Lim.”

Card 2 · The Third Brooch — The youngest sister is #5 Yap Eng Hooi (“died in childbirth 1923”). The blood granddaughter on the board tonight is #19 Khoo Hooi Wen. She inherits the third brooch by blood, though no one ever told her. Acceptable answer: “#19 — Khoo Hooi Wen is Eng Hooi's granddaughter.”

Card 3 · The Watch That Keeps Walking — The three Tan men: #1 Keo San (1891–1941) → his son Cheng Hoe (the firstborn legitimate son, in the registry) → his grandson alive tonight (the eldest male grandchild present — Bibi, point at him by name when you confirm). Acceptable answer: any naming of the three-generation male line of legitimate Tans.

Card 4 · The Cookbook Niece — The girl is Soo Hui. Her exact relation to Auntie Mei is niece (Auntie Mei's brother's daughter — a Lim by blood, not a Tan at all). Acceptable answer: “niece — yes she belongs, because she gave Auntie Mei her name back.” The argument itself is the point. Both sides are right.
